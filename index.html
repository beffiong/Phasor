01 — The Context

The AI data wall is real.
The next paradigm is already here.

High-quality training data for large language models is running out. Energy costs are unsustainable. The models that dominated the last decade were built on assumptions — infinite data, cheap compute — that are collapsing. The AI systems being built now are different: leaner, faster, designed to operate at the edge on novel neuromorphic and analog hardware. They don't need more of the internet. They need the right real-world data — and that data has, until now, barely existed.

02 — Episodes

Event-based datasets for
data-efficient models.

Episodes is a collection of real-world, event-based datasets designed to train Lean AI — models that learn to act from sparse, structured signals rather than massive text corpora. Each domain captures the kind of dynamic, time-sensitive data that embodied AI systems actually encounter: sensory events, physical interactions, environmental signals. This is training data built for models that need to do things, not just say things.

“We're not trying to build bigger training sets. We're building the right ones — for the models that will actually run the world.”
