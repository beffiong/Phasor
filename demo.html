Why neuromorphic data is different

Two cameras.
One scene.
Radically different data.

A standard camera works like a security guard who takes a photo every 33 milliseconds — whether anything is happening or not. Every frame is a complete picture: millions of pixels, most of them showing nothing new. That redundancy costs energy, storage, and time.

An event camera works differently. Each pixel fires only when it detects a change — like a motion sensor on every dot of the image. The result: 97% less data, microsecond response times, and perfect performance in darkness or fast motion where regular cameras blur and fail.

Phasor converts standard datasets into this event-based format — making them ready for the next generation of AI chips.
